Personas Geco

Personas Section (minimum 4 personas recommended)

Type: Primary

Name: James Jameson

Background
  • Date of Birth: 10/23/1990
  • Gender: Male
  • Location: Iasi, Romania
  • Work place: Awesome Company, Engineer
  • School: Palo Alto High School
Main Points
  • Experience with other smart assistant controlled by voice
  • Works in an office
  • Lives in a flat
Goals
  • Use gestural controlled decives easier
  • Control domotic devices with gestures
  • Reduce number of steps on doing things
  • Getting things done
Frustrations and Pain Points
  • Too many steps on doing some things
  • Possible overlap of gestures between devices
  • Doing gestures several times because the system didn't detect them
Detailed Description

James is an Engineer and it is a stressful job, so he wants to get things working at home as easy and intuitive as possible when he comes home after a hard day at the office, being always at least as fast as the way they work now. Also, the user doesn't want to feel stupid having to make a weird dance every time he wants to turn on the TV.

Scenarios

James arrives at home and, by using a couple of specific gestures, has his kitchen robot preparing dinner and the news on TV.

Type: Secondary

Name: Ivan Illich

Background
  • Date of Birth: 05/15/1974
  • Gender: Male
  • Location: Moscow, Russia
  • Work place: Illich Co., CEO
Main Points
  • Already using a traditional security system
  • Would be used by him and his family
  • Needs customizable gestures to be safer
Goals
  • Ensure his family and house
Frustrations and Pain Points
  • Activate system by mistake
  • Don't remember the gesture to unlock the system
  • Get "hacked" and getting robbed because someone guessed the gesture
Detailed Description

Ivan wants to use this device as security system on his door, to make his home more secure.
He lives in a dangerous city and wants his family to be safe against thieves and be woke up if something happens.
This system should also have a customizable gesture to be switched on/off, in order to be more difficult to be hacked.
